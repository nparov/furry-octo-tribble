Respiration
A ring of enzymes, ready to do their work.
A pyruvate comes to the ring of enzymes
NAD arrives to carry off hot hydrogens
Hot hydrogens go on to NAD carriers, making them NADH,
and carbons leave with 2 oxygen each.
[What happens to the extra carbon?]
Another enzyme is ready to help chemical reactions
A NADH molcule arrives at the enzyme
The enzyme takes the hydrogen atom off of the NADH,
turning it into NAD, which then leaves. The hydrogen atom is
split into an electron and a hydrogen ion and the electron goes
away, attached to a new carrier.
The enzyme puts the ion on the carrier with the electron
making it a hydrogen atom on the carrier.
The carrier carries the hydrogen to a new enzyme.
The enzyme takes the ions and electrons off of the carriers
The ions go through an enzyme...
(orange peeled to show inside of enzyme)
Which supplies energy to reattach phosphates to ATP molecules
At the enzyme that separates the ions from the electrons...
Oxygens come and carry off the hydrogen atoms
(the ions plus the electrons), making water
The End
